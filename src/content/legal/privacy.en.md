# Privacy Policy

*Last Updated: 18 May 2026*

*Status: Baseline content — pending legal review*

This Privacy Policy explains what personal data Dukanchi collects, why we collect it, how we use and protect it, and the rights you have over it. We have written this policy in plain language so that it is easy to understand. Please read it carefully.

## 1. Who We Are

Dukanchi is a hyperlocal retail-discovery app for India. It helps customers find nearby shops and products, and helps retailers, suppliers, brands, and manufacturers list what they offer and chat with customers.

Under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 ("DPDP Act 2023"), Dukanchi is a **Data Fiduciary**. This means we decide what personal data is collected and how it is used, and we are responsible for protecting it.

## 2. Information We Collect

We collect only the data we need to run the app. The main categories are:

- **Account information** — your name, phone number, password (stored only in a securely hashed form, never as plain text), your account role (customer, retailer, supplier, brand, or manufacturer), and the date your account was created. An email address is **optional**. You may also add an optional location as text.
- **Business verification (KYC)** — if you register a business account, we collect a photo of a government-issued ID document, a verification selfie, your intended store name and store photo, and the status and timestamps of your verification.
- **Listings and content** — for retailers and other business users: your stores and product posts, including photos, descriptions, and prices.
- **Location data** — saved locations and the location you choose to share so we can show you nearby shops and products.
- **Device and usage data** — basic technical information such as your IP address and browser or device type, plus product-analytics data collected through PostHog (for example, which pages you view and which features you use). We also keep your push-notification subscriptions and FCM tokens so we can send you notifications you have opted into.
- **Communications and interactions** — chat messages you send and receive, "Ask Nearby" stock-request enquiries, your follows, likes, saved items, **search history**, reviews, reports, and complaints.

> **No payment data.** Dukanchi does **not** process payments. We are a discovery and chat platform — any purchase happens offline, directly between you and the retailer. We do **not** collect payment-card details, order amounts, or transaction records.

## 3. Why We Collect This Information

We collect personal data for clear, limited purposes:

- **To create and run your account** — so you can sign in, use the app, and be identified.
- **To verify business accounts** — KYC data helps confirm that a retailer or supplier is genuine, which keeps the platform trustworthy.
- **To show relevant shops and products** — location and search data help us surface nearby and useful results.
- **To enable communication** — chat and "Ask Nearby" features let you contact retailers and check stock.
- **To improve the app** — analytics data helps us understand which features are useful and where the app can be better.
- **To keep the platform safe** — reports, complaints, and basic logs help us detect misuse and respond to it.
- **To send notifications** — only the push notifications you have opted into.

## 4. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data under the DPDP Act 2023:

- **Consent (Section 6)** — when you create an account, you give us consent to process your personal data for the purposes described in this policy. Your consent must be free, specific, and informed, and you can withdraw it at any time.
- **Certain legitimate uses (Section 7)** — in some situations, such as responding to a legal requirement or handling a complaint, we may process data without separate consent where the Act allows it.

We will not use your data for a new, unrelated purpose without telling you and, where required, asking for your consent again.

## 5. Your Rights as a Data Principal

As a **Data Principal** under the DPDP Act 2023 (Sections 11–14), you have the following rights:

- **Right to access (Section 11)** — you can ask for a summary of the personal data we hold about you and how we process it.
- **Right to correction and erasure (Section 12)** — you can ask us to correct data that is wrong or incomplete, and to erase data we no longer need to keep.
- **Right to grievance redressal (Section 13)** — you can raise a complaint with our Grievance Officer (see Section 11 below).
- **Right to nominate (Section 14)** — you can nominate another person to exercise your rights if you die or become unable to act.
- **Right to withdraw consent** — you can withdraw your consent at any time; this does not affect processing done before the withdrawal.

**How to exercise these rights:** use the in-app options in your Profile (for example, editing your details or deleting your account), or contact our Grievance Officer using the details in Section 11. We will respond within the timelines required by law.

## 6. How Long We Keep Your Information

We keep personal data only as long as it is needed. The table below summarises our retention periods:

| Data type | How long we keep it |
|---|---|
| Account data | Until you delete your account, plus a 30-day grace window |
| KYC documents (ID, selfie, store photo) | As long as needed for verification and to meet legal requirements |
| Technical logs | About 90 days |
| Analytics data (PostHog) | 2 years |
| Records we are legally required to retain (e.g. audit, dispute) | Kept as long as the law requires |
| Deleted-account residuals | Anonymised so they no longer identify you |

## 7. Who We Share Your Information With

We do not sell your personal data. We share data only with the service providers we need to run the app, and only to the extent required:

- **PostHog** — product analytics, hosted in the EU. See [PostHog's privacy policy](https://posthog.com/privacy).
- **Sentry** — error tracking; receives error stack traces and technical metadata only, with no intentional collection of personal data. See [Sentry's privacy policy](https://sentry.io/privacy/).
- **Google Maps** — loaded when you use the map or nearby-store search; Google may set its own cookies. See [Google's privacy policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy).
- **Future payment processors** — if Dukanchi adds payments in future, we will update this policy and name any payment processor before sharing data with them.
- **Law enforcement and authorities** — only when we are legally compelled to share data, such as a valid legal order.

## 8. Cross-Border Data Transfers

To run the app, some of your personal data is processed and stored outside India. We disclose this honestly:

- Application hosting is on Railway (United States).
- Our primary database is Neon Postgres (Singapore).
- Analytics is handled by PostHog (European Union).
- File storage uses Cloudflare R2.

This means your personal data may be processed in regions including Singapore, the United States, and the European Union. We choose providers that apply strong data-protection practices.

## 9. Children's Privacy

Dukanchi is intended for adults. Under Section 9 of the DPDP Act 2023, the personal data of a child (a person under 18) may only be processed with **verifiable consent of a parent or lawful guardian**. We do not knowingly process a child's data without such consent.

Retailers and other business users **must be 18 or older** and legally able to run a business in India. If we learn that we hold a child's data without proper consent, we will delete it.

## 10. How We Protect Your Information

We take reasonable security measures to protect your personal data:

- All data in transit is protected with HTTPS/TLS encryption.
- Passwords are stored only in a securely hashed form (bcrypt), never as plain text.
- Authentication uses httpOnly cookies, which cannot be read by JavaScript in the browser.
- Account deletion uses a soft-delete with a 30-day grace window, and session tokens are rotated regularly.
- Access to personal data is limited to people who need it to operate the service.

No system can be guaranteed 100% secure. While we work hard to protect your data, we cannot promise absolute security.
