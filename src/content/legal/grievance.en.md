# Grievance Officer

*Last Updated: 18 May 2026*

*Status: Baseline content — pending legal review*

This page tells you how to raise a data-protection complaint with Dukanchi and what to expect after you do.

## 1. About This Role

Under Section 10 of the DPDP Act 2023, every Data Fiduciary must appoint a **Grievance Officer**. The Grievance Officer is the point of contact for Data Principals (the people whose data is processed) who want to raise a complaint or concern about how their personal data is handled.

Dukanchi has appointed a Grievance Officer for exactly this purpose.

## 3. How to File a Complaint

You can file a complaint by **email or by post** using the details above. To help us deal with your complaint quickly, please include:

- **Your name.**
- **The phone number or email registered on your Dukanchi account**, so we can identify you.
- **A clear description of your grievance** — what happened and what your concern is.
- **The resolution you are seeking** — what you would like us to do.

## 4. Response Timeline

We take grievances seriously. We aim to acknowledge and respond to your complaint **within 30 days** of receiving it, in line with the DPDP Act 2023.

## 5. Escalation: Data Protection Board of India

If you are not satisfied with how we have handled your grievance, you can escalate the matter to the **Data Protection Board of India**, the authority set up under the DPDP Act 2023.

> You can find more information about the Board at <https://dpdp.gov.in>.
